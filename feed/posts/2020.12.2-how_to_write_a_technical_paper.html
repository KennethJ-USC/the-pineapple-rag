Tuesday Commentary: How to Write a Technical Paper

Link to original article:

Target Article:

How to Write a Technical Paper
by Georgios Varsamopoulos


Introduction
I chose this short paper by ASU's Georgios Varsamopoulos for two reasons: (1) the topic of the paper, and (2) how this paper demonstrates its own salience.

Or put another way (less obnoxiously): First, it is an underappreciated topic. Writing well, especially authoring a technical paper, is an intimidating and often underappreciated obstacles for many young researchers and technical minds. While we aren't squarely in the business of science here in ITDA, we can all appreciate the value of a well written white-paper, or a user error report that actually includes all the critical information It seems that writing is often the best tool to achieve consensus on a complex idea.

In my opinion, the second point - "this paper demonstrates its own salience" - is more interesting. The form of the paper takes the shape that its content recommends. It is a template for a technical paper as much as an set of instructions. Really mind blowing stuff, I'm sure you agree...

This second article is a great 5-minute read (only 2 pages!). I debated saving it as a dedicated post, but figured it made the most sense to leave in here. I think that studying the ways others think - especially how they think about hard or complex problems, such as retaining the information in the research paper - improves the subject's ability to reason about problems in general. Admittedly, I'm not sure I have the rigorous evidence to back that up, but I digress.
